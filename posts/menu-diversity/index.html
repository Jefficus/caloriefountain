    Menu Diversity

    Jefferson Smith

    2023-02-27 14:18

    Comments

    The primary purpose of a calorie fountain is to provide enough calories to sustain a family when other sources of food are either too expensive or unavailable, but there are three distinctt modes of operation, depending on the severity of the situation...

    In the most extreme mode (what I call “crisis” mode) we're not concerned with how good the food tastes, how completed the nutritional profile is, or how boring the menu might be. It's about squeezing every calorie possible out of what little space you have. It may not be fun, but you'll still be alive to complain about it.

    As you might guess, crisis mode is intended for short, high-stress situations only. As soon as possible, you should revert your fountain to “balanced” mode.

    In balanced mode, your fountain will take up more room, but it will provide a more complete nutritional profile as well, including most of the minerals and vitamins recommended by the national nutrition guidelines. This is where you want to be if you're relying predominantly on your fountain for your nutrition. How exciting your menu might be will depend on how much space you have to work with and how creative you can be with a limited set of ingredients to work with.

    Another solution, if you have friends or neighbors with fountains of their own, would be to coordinate your community to produce a wider variety of crops and share them.

    And lastly, there's “supplement” mode, where you use your fountain is not your primary food source and you're using it to supplement your diet with fresh ingredients.

    But be aware that there are no actual dials or switches or buttons you press to switch from one mode to another. A calorie fountain is just a calorie fountain, and it will grow whatever crops you decide to grow. These "modes" are just terms we use to encapsulate some common scenarios a fountaineer might find themselves in, and to describe how the system might be tuned to support them.

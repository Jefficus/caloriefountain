    Target "Kratky How"

    Jefferson Smith

    2023-03-24 10:59

    GOAL: Determine a solid methodology for designing Kratky systems, optimizing plant densities, water volume, and nutrient formulas.

    Explanation

    We need to be able to design tank setups for the specific needs of the crop being grown.

    Deliverables

    • a method for determining optimal seedling layout
    • a method for computing initial water volume
    • a method for computing initial nutrient mix

    Success test

    • a Kratky tank designed with these rubrics produced a successful, full-term crop

    This target is more advanced than I need for initial crop proving tests. I'm going to pause it until my initial naive tests are complete.

    Nutrient mix

    What seems to be common practice is mixing Masterblend (in a standard ratio with Epsom Salt and Calcium Nitrate) at a standard proportion per gallon of water.

    Water

    I haven't seen any rubrics yet, but a first approximation occurs to me: the container should be at least deep enough to contain the typical root depth and have a lid size at least equal to the recommended spacing for seedlings. So a container for a single plant on 4" spacing would have a lid 4x4, while a container for 12 plants at 3" spacing would measure 9"x12".

    Since the starting water level needs to keep the seedling moistened, sizing the container completely dictates the amount of water the system starts with.

    At the very least, sizing containers this way should provide sufficient volume for the plant to not be cramped. However, it ignores the possibility that growing in water will have different space requirements than the norms established by a history of growing in soil.

    It also ignores the possibility that some plants might require more water over their growth cycle than will fit in that initial root-defined volume. Conventional Kratky wisdom is to check the water levels regulary and top them up as needed, but I'd prefer a methodology that models and predicts water needs more accurately. In the worst case, we should be able to collect water requirements for each target crop during the crop proving phase, such as Targetsalsifyprove and Targetseaweedprove.
